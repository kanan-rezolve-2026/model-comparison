📊 Metrics Interpretation Guide

⚡ Latency
Lower is better.
Measures generation time.
Fast models are preferred for real-time applications.
🎯 CLIP Score
Higher is better (0–1).
Measures how well output matches the prompt.
Higher = better semantic understanding.
✨ Aesthetic Score
Higher is better.
Represents visual appeal and realism.
Higher = more visually pleasing output.
🧩 SSIM
Higher is better (0–1).
Measures structure similarity with reference image.
Higher = better preservation.
📈 PSNR
Higher is better.
Measures pixel-level reconstruction quality.
Used for fine-grained comparison.
🧠 LPIPS
Lower is better.
Measures perceptual difference.
Lower = more visually similar.
🎬 Motion Score
Measures movement between frames.
Moderate = natural motion.
Very high = unstable video.
🔄 Consistency
Higher is better.
Measures temporal stability.
Higher = smoother video.
🖼 First Frame SSIM
Higher is better.
Checks identity preservation.
Higher = closer to input image.
🧪 First Frame LPIPS
Lower is better.
Measures perceptual similarity to input.
Lower = better match.
📏 Resolution
Image size (e.g., 1024×1024).
Higher = more detail.
Does not always mean better quality.

🏆 How to Choose the Best Model:

✔ High CLIP → follows prompt
✔ High Aesthetic → looks good
✔ High SSIM + Low LPIPS → best edits
✔ Smooth Motion + High Consistency → best videos
